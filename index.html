Test Details

  
This test consists of four exercises, each scored on a 0-100 scale. The final score is the average of all four event scores.


  
Exercises & Scoring

  
    
    
  • 
      Pull-Ups: A test of upper body strength.
      
    Start from a dead hang on a pull-up bar, then pull yourself up until your chin is above the bar. Lower yourself back to a full hang before starting the next rep.
    
      
    Scoring: 5 points per pull-up, up to a maximum of 20 reps (100 points).
    
    
    • 

    
  • 
      Push-Ups: Measures muscular endurance.
      
    Perform as many full push-ups as possible in 2 minutes. A proper push-up starts in a plank position, lowering your chest to the ground and pushing back up.
    
      
    Scoring: A linear scale from 0 to 80 reps, calculated as (reps × 100 / 80), rounded down to the nearest 0.5. Maximum score at 80 reps.
    
    
    • 

    
  • 
      Plank Hold: Evaluates core endurance.
      
    Hold a plank position on your forearms and toes, keeping your body straight. Time is measured in minutes and seconds.
    
      
    Scoring: Exponential scaling formula: 
     
      100 × (1 - e-1.2 × (time in minutes)). 
    
      Higher times result in diminishing point increases. 7+ minutes earns the full 100 points.
    
    
    • 

    
  • 
      2-Mile Run: Tests cardiovascular fitness.
      
    Run a distance of 2 miles (3.2 km) as fast as possible. Time is recorded in minutes and seconds.
    
      
    Scoring: 100 points for a time of 12:00 minutes or faster. Every 30 seconds slower deducts 5 points, calculated as:
    
      100 - 5 × ((time in seconds - 720) / 30). 
    
      The lowest possible score is 0 at 22:00+ minutes.
    
    
    • 
  


  
Final Score Calculation

  
Final Score = (Pull-Up Score + Push-Up Score + Plank Score + Run Score) / 4.
